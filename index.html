Truth #1

“CPF interest is 4%”… really?

Your Retirement Account (RA) earns a floor rate before payouts. But from age 65, your RA is usually used to buy CPF LIFE— converting savings into lifetime income. That means you don’t see 4% keep compounding in your account; the interest is embedded in the annuity that funds your payouts.

The real, long-horizon effective rate from age 55 to 85 is ~2–3%+. Why? There’s a 10-year wait (55→65) with no cashflows, followed by fixed monthly payouts (65→85). Under realistic cohort assumptions, the annualised internal rate works out in the ~2.5% to ~3.5% range depending on plan and payout level—not a visible 4% compounding balance.

Illustrative and for education; exact numbers depend on your balances, plan (Standard/Escalating), and start age.

Why this matters

1
Don’t mistake lifetime income for ongoing visible compounding in RA.
2
Inflation risk persists—consider Escalating payouts and an external growth sleeve.
3
Coordinate CPF with low-volatility instruments (cash/T-Bills/SRS) for flexibility.

A smarter liquidity plan

1
Pre-fund a 3–5 year “rainy-day” cash ladder outside CPF before payouts start.
2
Layer income: CPF LIFE + SRS drawdown + other predictable cashflow assets.
3
Time larger expenses (renovations, gifts) before annuitisation where feasible.
Truth #2

Liquidity after 65 is limited

Once CPF LIFE payouts begin, most RA savings are committed to lifetime income. Ad-hoc lump-sum access is constrained. You can still have limited withdrawals in specific circumstances, but the annuity is designed to pay monthly—not to be cashed out on demand.

Practical takeaway: treat CPF LIFE as your longevity income engine, and build liquidity outside it.

Truth #3

Bequest: “If I pass at 85, what’s left?”

CPF LIFE pays you for as long as you live. Because income is being paid out over time, the bequest typically reduces and can reach near-zero at advanced ages once payouts exceed what went in—this is how lifelong annuities fund longevity protection.

If legacy is important, ring-fence a separate bequest bucket outside your lifetime-income engine.

Protect your legacy

1
Maintain a legacy portfolio (diversified, low-vol) outside CPF LIFE.
2
Use nominations/titling so beneficiaries don’t face delays or complexity.
3
Match plan choice (Standard/Escalating) to inflation and legacy priorities.
